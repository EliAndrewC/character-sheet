# Shosuro Actor 5th Dan: marginal value of an extra rolled die

For each `(rolled, kept)` dice pool the table below shows:

- **avg roll** - the mean of an `XkY` roll.
- **Shosuro avg bonus** - the mean sum of the lowest three rolled
  dice (the Shosuro Actor 5th Dan bonus). For `rolled < 3` the
  "lowest 3" rule degenerates to "all of them", so the bonus equals
  the average sum of all rolled dice.
- **gain from +1k0** - how much the average roll goes up if you add
  one more rolled die to the pool (kept fixed).
- **Shosuro change per +1 rolled** - signed delta on the Shosuro
  bonus from adding one rolled die. Positive = the bonus goes UP
  (only happens on the `rolled = 2 -> 3` transition). Negative =
  the bonus goes DOWN, the usual case.
- **net +1k0** - sum of the previous two columns. The expected
  total swing in your result from adding `+1k0` when the Shosuro
  5th Dan ability is in play.

The `rolled = 10` rows have no `+1k0` columns: the L7R overflow
rule (`rolled > 10 -> kept += overflow`) prevents an 11th rolled
die. See `analysis/ShosuroFifthDanBonusesByPercentile.md` for the
distributional spread (10th / 50th / 90th percentile) of the same
quantities.

Generated by `analysis/shosuro_fifth_dan_bonuses.py` from the
precomputed Monte Carlo tables in `app/data/`.
